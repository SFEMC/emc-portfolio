You have teams. You have systems. You have processes documented in SharePoint folders that nobody opens. What you don't have is someone who owns the experience of using your services.

Pick any service that a University provides. Chances are it spans five or six teams. No single one owns the end-to-end experience. Nobody has a unified budget for it. Nobody reports on it.

That's a gap that keeps showing up across higher education and it's where students drop off, staff build workarounds and the same problems come back to haunt you every cycle.

I wrote about what changes when you stop designing around internal processes and start thinking in services.
