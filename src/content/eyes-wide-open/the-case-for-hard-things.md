title: "The Case for Hard Things"
collection: "Eyes Wide Open"

Everything around you is built to make life easier. Most of it is making you weaker. The food finds you. The entertainment never runs out. The cold is one tap from warm, the far is one tap from near, the hard is one tap from done. We have spent a century removing friction from human life. We are the first people who have to go looking for difficulty on purpose, because none arrives on its own any more.

Comfort hides one thing from you. You are built to be used. A muscle you do not strain wastes. A mind you never stretch goes soft. A person who is never tested has no idea what they are. They suspect the worst. The body and the mind both follow one rule: give them resistance and they grow to meet it, take the resistance away and they shrink to fit. Ease is not neutral. It costs you, in capability you do not notice losing until you need it.

So you have to put the hard back in on purpose. Not suffering for its own sake, that is a different and sadder thing. Chosen difficulty, the kind you walk toward. The cold shower you dread and never regret. The run that starts awful. The conversation you would rather avoid. The skill that humbles you for months. Each one is small. Each one teaches the same lesson your comfortable life keeps hiding. You can do hard things. The doing remakes you a little.

The reward is not the stronger body or the new skill alone, though you get those. The real prize is who you become by choosing the hard road when the easy one was right there. Confidence is not something you talk yourself into. It is the residue of having done difficult things, the quiet knowledge, earned and not borrowed, that when the next hard thing comes you will meet it. You cannot get that from comfort. Comfort has nothing to teach you about yourself.

There is a strange mercy in this too. The hard thing you are avoiding is often the exact thing that would free you. The fitness you lack, the talk you keep ducking, the work you know you should be doing: the discomfort is a door. On the far side of it is a version of you no longer afraid of that particular thing. Avoid it and the fear stays. Walk through it and the fear has nowhere to live.

So choose something hard this week and do it, even if you are bad at it. The cold water, the heavy thing, the page that will not come, the call you are dreading. Pain is not the point. Growth is. It was always kept on the far side of the hard thing. The soft life feels like a gift. It is a slow trade, your strength for your ease. Take some of it back.
