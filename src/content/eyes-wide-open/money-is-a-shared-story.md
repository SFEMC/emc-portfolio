title: "Money Is a Shared Story"

Take a banknote out of your pocket and look at it. It is a piece of paper, or now a line of code on a screen, worth nothing in itself. You cannot eat it, burn it for much heat or build with it. It has value for one reason. Everyone agrees it does. The whole of money rests on a story we all decided to believe at the same time. The story works as long as nobody stops believing it.

This is easy to wave away until you sit with it. The pound in your account is not backed by gold in a vault. It is backed by your faith that tomorrow the shop will still take it. The shop trusts its supplier will. On it goes, a chain of trust with nothing solid at the bottom. When that faith breaks, the money does not lose some value. It turns back into paper in an afternoon, as people have watched happen in country after country when the story collapsed.

None of this means money is fake or that you should ignore it. The story is real in its effects, the way the rules of a game are real while you are playing. You cannot opt out. Try living a week without touching the shared fiction and you will starve inside a system you never signed up for. The point is not to escape it. The point is to see it for what it is, because seeing it changes how it holds you.

Here is what changes. When you forget money is a story, you treat it as a scoreboard for your worth. The number going up or down feels like you going up or down. When you remember it is a tool built on shared trust, you can use it hard without letting it tell you who you are. It buys time, safety, choices, the room to be generous. Those are real. The deep human stuff it is supposed to stand in for, the love, the meaning, the worth, it cannot touch, because those were never priced in the story.

So hold money the way you hold any powerful tool. Respect it, because the story is enforced and the consequences of ignoring it are real and hard. Use it, because it buys the things that protect the people you love. But do not worship it. Do not let a number in a shared story become the measure of a life it was never built to measure.

The richest people I can think of are not the ones with the most of the story. They are the ones who have enough of it to stop thinking about it, who never confused the scoreboard with the game.
