Your attention is the most valuable thing you own. You are giving it away for free, all day, to people who sell it on. That is the business now. The biggest companies on earth do not charge you money. They harvest your attention, package it and sell it to whoever wants to put something in front of you. You are not the customer. You are the field they farm.

This matters more than it sounds, because attention is not one resource among many. It is the one your whole life is made of. What you pay attention to is what you experience. What you experience, added up, is your life. The person who controls your attention controls the raw material of your days. When you hand it to a feed designed to keep it, you are not wasting time. You are handing over pieces of your one life to be spent on someone else's profit.

And it does not give the pieces back. The hours go, but worse than the hours is what the constant pull does to the muscle itself. A mind interrupted a hundred times a day learns to interrupt itself. You sit down to read and reach for the phone before the page turns. You lose the ability to stay with one thing long enough for it to get deep. Depth is where everything good lives, the work that matters, the conversation that lands, the idea that needed an hour of quiet to arrive. Trade your attention away and you do not lose time. You lose the depth that made the time worth having.

The good news is that attention is a muscle. Muscles come back. You take it back the way you lost it, a habit at a time. Put the phone in another room, not in your pocket, because willpower loses to a thing within reach. Do one thing at a time and let it be boring. Read a long thing to the end. Sit with a hard problem past the point your hand twitches for the escape. Each time you hold your focus on purpose, you take back a little of the ground. The ability to go deep returns.

Guard it like the treasure it is. When something asks for your attention, it is asking for a piece of your life. Most of the things asking do not deserve it. Spend it on the people, the work and the wonder that do. Attention is not part of the game. It is the whole game. You are the one who decides where it goes, if you can take it back long enough to choose.
