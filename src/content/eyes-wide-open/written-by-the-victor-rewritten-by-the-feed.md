title: "Written by the Victor, Rewritten by the Feed"

You have heard that history is written by the victors. It is a good line. Nobody can quite say who first said it. People hand it to Churchill, though he never wrote it. Versions turn up in French and Italian long before his time. The line proves its own point. Even the quote about history being rewritten has been rewritten.

For most of the past, the winners wrote the record and then it set hard. A book was a book. Once it sat on a shelf in a thousand homes, no one could reach into all of them at midnight and change a paragraph. The lie, if there was one, at least held still long enough to be caught.

The old masters of the rewrite worked with scissors and paint. In Stalin's Soviet Union, men who fell out of favour fell out of the photographs too. David King's book The Commissar Vanishes collects the evidence. Nikolai Yezhov ran the secret police and stood beside Stalin at the Moscow canal, then he was shot in 1940 and airbrushed from the picture, replaced by a stretch of empty water. One day he was history. The next he was a puddle. Even that took a darkroom, a scalpel and an artist with a steady hand.

Now the record is liquid. A news site can change a headline an hour after you read it. The version you saw is gone with no trace it existed. An article gets edited after publication with no note that anything moved. A post is deleted, a statement walked back, a definition updated while you were not looking. The past is no longer written and shelved. It is a live document. Whoever holds the login holds the pen.

Most of this is not a grand conspiracy. It is a thousand small edits, each with a reason, adding up to a past you cannot trust to stay still. The strange part is that the same tool that broke the record is the one keeping a copy. The Wayback Machine and a handful of archives are the nearest thing we have to a fixed shelf now, run by volunteers, saving what the rest of the internet keeps revising.

So what do you do. You stop treating what you read today as final. When something matters, you save it, screenshot it, note the date. You check whether the page you are arguing about still says what you think it said. You learn the unglamorous habit this whole collection keeps coming back to: find the source and check it before you carry the claim to anyone else.

History was always written by the winners. The new thing is that the winners can edit yesterday by tea time. They do not need a victory to do it. They need an account and a reason. Your defence is the same as it ever was. Now you use it more often. Doubt the clean story. Look for what is missing. Keep your own copy of the truth.

*Sources*

- David King, The Commissar Vanishes: The Falsification of Photographs and Art in Stalin's Russia (1997). Yezhov was airbrushed from the canal photograph after his execution in 1940.
- "History is written by the victors" has no firm origin. The Churchill attribution is undocumented. Earlier French and Italian versions exist.
- The Internet Archive Wayback Machine and similar archives act as the de facto record of pages that later change or vanish.
