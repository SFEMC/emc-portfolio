---
title: "What Is Life"
collection: "Eyes Wide Open"
status: "published"
type: "poem"
---

What is life about?
I have asked it all the way through this book.
The answer keeps coming back small.

It is trying.
Getting up and having a go
at the thing you are not sure you can do.

It is striving.
Wanting more than you have,
reaching past your own arm's length,
not for the having but for the reach.

It is failing.
Most of it, if we are honest.
The red lights, the closed doors,
the plans that came apart in your hands.
Failing is not the opposite of living.
It is most of how it is done.

It is smiling.
At a stranger. At the milk and the morning.
At the whole improbable fact of being here at all.

It is laughing.
The real kind, that bends you over,
that you cannot fake and cannot plan,
that says for a second nothing is wrong.

It is learning.
Right to the end.
Staying a beginner. Letting a thing
click today that would not click yesterday.

That is the list.
Not the money, which was a story.
Not the feed, which was a thief.
Not the team you were told to hate.
Those were never it.

It was the people.
It was the green lights and the blessing of the red.
It was the one life you were handed,
free, before you had earned a thing.
It was spending that life awake.

You are going to die.
So is everyone you love.
That is not the sad part.
That is the part that makes the morning matter.

So try. Strive. Fail. Smile. Laugh. Learn.
Then do it again tomorrow,
with your eyes wide open,
for as long as you are lucky enough to get.
