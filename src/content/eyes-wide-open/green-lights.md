Matthew McConaughey wrote a memoir called Greenlights. The whole book turns on a simple picture. Life is a road of traffic lights. Green means go, yes, the thing you wanted arrived. We chase green all day, the job, the yes, the open road. We treat every red and amber as the road failing us. He says something braver than that. Given enough time, the reds and the yellows turn green too.

Hold the idea up against your own life and it holds. Think of the worst red light you ever sat at. The job you did not get. The person who left. The plan that fell apart in your hands and took a year of you with it. At the time it was pure loss, a stop with no reason and no end. Now look back from here. More often than not, that red light routed you onto the road you are glad to be on.

You can do this with your own. The heartbreak you were sure would end you did not end you. It rearranged you. It taught you what you would not stand for, or it pointed you at the person or the work that came next. The pain was real. It was also, though you could not see it then, a green light wearing a red coat.

This is not a trick for pretending bad things are good. A red light is still a stop. Some of them cost more than you would ever choose to pay. The heartbreak hurt. The failure was a failure. The point is not that the pain was a gift in disguise. The point is that you cannot read a light from the spot where you are sitting at it. Time moves you down the road. From further on you can see where it sent you.

So what do you do with that, today, at the light you are stuck at now. Two things. You keep chasing green, because a life is built by going after the things you want, not by waiting for the road to clear. And you stop treating every red as a verdict on you. The amber you are cursing this week may be the bend that saves you. You will not know for years. That is the deal.

The hardest greens to count are the ones still dressed as red. The thing falling apart in your hands right now, the door that closed, the no you did not want. Sit at it if you have to. Feel it. Then drive on, because the way to find out what colour it was is to keep moving.

Chase the green. Bless the red. Most of them are the same light, caught at different times.
