You did not earn the most important thing about your life. You did not choose the country you were born in, the century, the language that shaped your thoughts before you had any. You did not pick your parents, your body, the money or lack of it around your cot, the wars you missed or did not. By the time you were old enough to decide anything, the dice had already been thrown. Most of your life was sitting in the result.

We do not like this. We prefer the story where we built ourselves, where the credit is ours. The effort is real. You did work, you did choose, you did push. But run the experiment. Take the exact you, the same drive and the same gifts. Drop you into a slum in a failing state, or a century with no medicine, or a body that does not work. Same person. A different draw. The life is gone. You were handed most of what you call yourself, free, before you had done a thing to deserve it.

Sit with that and it gives you two things at once. The first is humility. It gets harder to look down on anyone when you know the gap between you and them is, in large part, the gap between two draws neither of you chose. The man who annoys you for failing might have started from a place you would not have survived.

The second is patience, with others and with yourself. The people you envy drew a good hand in ways they cannot see. The people you judge drew a poor one in ways they cannot help. Once you see how much was luck, the urge to envy and the urge to judge both lose their grip.

None of this means you are off the hook. This is the part people get wrong. Knowing how much of your life was luck is not a reason to stop trying. It is a reason to carry what you were given with some grace. You were lucky. The right response to luck is not guilt, which helps nobody. It is gratitude. And then a kind of duty: to use a hand you did not earn as well as you can and to go a little easier on the people who drew worse.

You woke up today as the luckiest kind of thing there is, a person who gets to notice any of this at all. That was the biggest win on the ticket. Most of the draws went the other way.
