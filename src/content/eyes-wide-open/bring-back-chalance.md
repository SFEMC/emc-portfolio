---
title: "Bring Back Chalance"

There is no such word as chalance. We kept the negative, nonchalant, the one that means cool and unbothered and above it all. We threw the root away. That feels about right for the age. We have a word for not caring and no word for its opposite, because not caring is the look everyone is going for.

Watch how people talk now. You are not allowed to want things too much. You hedge. You say you are "not fussed", you "might" go, it is "whatever", before anyone can catch you caring and use it against you. Enthusiasm has become a little embarrassing. To be keen is to be uncool. The safest pose is the shrug, the half-interest, the love kept quiet enough that nobody can mock it.

I understand why. If you never admit you wanted it, it cannot hurt when you do not get it. Nonchalance is armour. Care about nothing out loud and nothing out loud can wound you. But look at the cost. A whole life spent pretending not to want things is a life half-lived, all the colour turned down so nobody sees you flinch. You protect yourself from disappointment by giving up the wanting that makes anything worth having.

So bring back chalance. Not the word, the thing. The nerve to care out loud, to be caught wanting it, to say this matters to me and stand there while people see that it does. To clap at the gig. To be the one who organised it and minded whether it went well. To love a thing in the open and risk looking a fool.

The people worth being around are the ones who never got the memo about staying cool. The friend delighted by his strange hobby who does not care who knows. The one who cries at the film and the wedding and does not apologise for it. The person who throws himself into the small thing, the meal, the trip, the birthday, as though it mattered, because to him it does. They are not naive. They have decided caring beats the cool alternative. A life spent guarding against looking keen is a poor trade.

Nonchalance keeps you safe the way a locked room keeps you safe. Nothing can hurt you. Nothing can reach you either.

The cure is not to care about everything. It is to find the few things that are yours and care about them without the apology, the hedge or the shrug. Let people see it. Be too much. Bring back chalance. Wear it where they can see.
