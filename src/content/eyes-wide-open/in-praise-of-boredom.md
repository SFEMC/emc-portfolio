We killed boredom. We are starting to see what it was for. A few years ago there were gaps in every day with nothing in them. The wait for the bus. The queue. The ten minutes before sleep. The long car journey staring out of the window. We filled all of them with the phone, every last one. It felt like a clear upgrade. No more dead time. We were wrong about what that time was doing.

Boredom was not empty. It was the mind left unstimulated long enough to do its own thing. Its own thing turns out to matter. When you stop feeding it input, the mind does not switch off. It wanders. It connects things that were sitting apart. It chews on the problem you had given up on and hands you the answer in the shower. Almost every good idea you have ever had arrived in a gap, not while you were staring at a screen but while you were walking, washing up, drifting off. Those gaps are where thinking happens. We paved over all of them.

There is a second loss, quieter than the first. The ability to be bored is the ability to be still. A mind that cannot tolerate a single empty minute has lost something. Watch a child handed a phone the instant they fidget. They never learn the small, useful art of sitting with nothing and being fine. We are all that child now, reaching for the screen the moment the world stops entertaining us. We are forgetting how to be somewhere, doing nothing, with our own mind for company.

So be bored on purpose. Leave the phone in your pocket in the queue. Take the walk with nothing in your ears. Lie awake for the ten minutes before sleep and let the mind run loose. It will feel like wasting time. It is the opposite. You are giving your mind back the empty space it needs to do the work it can do when you leave it alone at last.
