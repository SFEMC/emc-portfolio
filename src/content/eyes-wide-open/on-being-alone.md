There is a difference between being alone and being lonely. We have started to forget it. Lonely is a lack, the ache of wanting company you do not have. Solitude is a choice, the deliberate, nourishing act of being in your own company on purpose. One is something done to you. The other is something you do for yourself. We have made them sound like the same thing, so people now treat any time alone as a problem to fix with a screen.

Watch how hard we work to never be alone with ourselves. The moment a queue forms, the phone comes out. The walk has a podcast, the meal has a screen, the silence gets filled before it can settle. We have engineered away the gaps where a person used to be alone with their own thoughts. We are surprised those thoughts now feel like strangers. You cannot know your own mind if you never sit in it without noise.

There is a cost to never learning solitude that goes deeper than missing thoughts. If you cannot bear your own company, you are not free. You will cling to people, not because you love them but because they keep the silence away. That need warps every relationship you have. You will stay where you should leave. You will say yes to avoid an evening alone. The person who is fine on their own can choose company without fear, because they are not running from anything when they pick it.

So practise it. Solitude is a skill. Like any skill it is strange before it is good. Sit without the phone. Walk with nothing in your ears. Eat a meal with the meal and nothing else. The first few times your mind will scramble for the exit, because it is out of practice. Stay. On the other side of the restlessness is something we have almost lost, a quiet hour with yourself that leaves you steadier than any amount of company could.

You came in alone. You will go out alone. The hours in between go better if you make peace with the one person you can never leave. Be good company for yourself. You will never be lonely in the deep way again, no matter who is or is not in the room.
